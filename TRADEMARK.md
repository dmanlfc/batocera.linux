# Batocera Linux Trademark Policy

## Purpose

The Batocera Linux name, logos, artwork, and visual identity exist so that users can confidently identify software that has been produced, tested, and released by the Batocera Linux Project.

Protecting the Batocera Linux identity helps reduce confusion between official releases and independently maintained derivative projects.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- The name "Batocera Linux"
- Official logos
- Official artwork
- Official visual branding
- Official project identity

---

## Permitted Uses

You may:

- Refer to official Batocera Linux releases by name
- State that a project is "based on Batocera Linux"
- Reference Batocera Linux in documentation, articles, reviews, or educational materials
- Link to official Batocera Linux resources

---

## Derivative Projects

Derivative projects may describe themselves as being based on Batocera Linux.

However, derivative projects should not present themselves as official Batocera Linux releases.

---

## Prohibited Uses

Without explicit written permission from the Batocera Linux Project, you may not:

- Use the Batocera Linux name as the primary name of a modified distribution
- Use official Batocera Linux logos in derivative projects
- Imply endorsement, certification, sponsorship, or affiliation
- Misrepresent unofficial builds as official releases

---

## Fork Naming

Forks and derivative projects should adopt their own distinct names and branding.

This protects users from confusion and preserves the integrity of official Batocera Linux releases.

---

## Official Status

Only releases distributed through official Batocera Linux Project channels may claim official status.

The project reserves the right to publicly clarify whether a release, build, distribution, or service is official or unofficial.

---

## Intent

This policy is intended to protect users and preserve project identity.

It is not intended to restrict rights granted by GPLv3 or other open-source licenses.
