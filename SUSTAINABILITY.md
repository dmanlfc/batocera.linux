# Batocera Linux Sustainability and Funding

## Purpose

The long-term success of Batocera Linux depends upon sustainable development, infrastructure, maintenance, and community participation.

This document describes the project's approach to funding and sustainability.

---

## Principles

The Batocera Linux Project is guided by:

- Transparency
- Fairness
- Sustainability
- Stewardship
- Community benefit

---

## Sources of Support

Support for Batocera Linux may include:

- Donations
- Sponsorships
- Infrastructure support
- Hardware donations
- Grants
- Community fundraising

---

## Use of Funds

Project resources may be used for:

- Infrastructure
- Build systems
- Hosting
- Development hardware
- Testing hardware
- Documentation
- Security work
- Release engineering
- Contributor recognition
- Community initiatives

---

## Contributor Recognition

The Batocera Linux Project recognizes that contributors invest substantial time and expertise into the project.

Where resources permit, contributors may receive support, recognition, or assistance based upon project needs and available funding.

No contribution automatically creates entitlement to compensation, ownership, revenue sharing, or future payments.

---

## Financial Decisions

Financial decisions should prioritize:

- Long-term sustainability
- Project health
- Infrastructure stability
- User benefit
- Community growth

---

## Transparency

The project should strive for reasonable transparency regarding the use of community-provided resources while respecting privacy, security, and legal obligations.

---

## Shared Responsibility

The sustainability of Batocera Linux is a shared responsibility between maintainers, contributors, sponsors, and users.

The project encourages all participants to support the ecosystem in ways appropriate to their abilities and circumstances.
